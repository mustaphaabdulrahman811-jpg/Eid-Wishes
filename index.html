Eid Around The World

Discover how over 1.8 billion Muslims across every continent celebrate Eid with joy, unity, and gratitude.

🇳🇬

Nigeria

Eid in Nigeria is a massive communal celebration! Muslims don vibrant Agbada, Kaftan & Hijabs. Mosques overflow with Eid prayers, followed by joyful family feasts with Jollof Rice, Suya, and Puff-Puff. Durbar festivals in the North feature magnificent horse parades.

🎊 Durbar Festival 🍚 Jollof Rice 🐎 Horse Parades 👘 Agbada
🇲🇦

Morocco

Moroccan Eid is rich with tradition. Families sacrifice a lamb (Eid al-Adha), while women wear the elegant Djellaba. Streets are adorned with colorful lanterns, and homes fill with the aroma of Bastilla, Harira soup, and sweet Sellou pastries.

🕌 Grand Mosques 🥙 Harira 👘 Djellaba 🏮 Lanterns
🇪🇬

Egypt

Egypt celebrates Eid with Al-Azhar prayers and family visits. The iconic Ka'ak biscuits are gifted house to house. Children receive Eidiyya money and wear new clothes. Streets come alive with music, swings, and fairground rides along the Nile.

🕌 Al-Azhar 🍪 Ka'ak 💰 Eidiyya 🎡 Fairgrounds
🇸🇳

Senegal

Senegalese Muslims celebrate Eid with profound spirituality. Women wear striking Grand Boubou garments with elaborate embroidery. Communities gather for Thiéboudienne fish rice and share gifts. Sufi brotherhoods add a unique musical and spiritual dimension.

👗 Grand Boubou 🎵 Sufi Music 🍽️ Thiéboudienne 🤝 Unity
🇸🇦

Saudi Arabia

The birthplace of Islam celebrates Eid grandly. Prayers at Masjid al-Haram in Makkah draw millions. Families exchange gifts of gold jewellery and dates. Traditional Kabsa rice with roasted meat graces every table amidst fireworks and street celebrations.

🕋 Haram🍖 Kabsa🎆 Fireworks
🇵🇰

Pakistan

Eid in Pakistan bursts with color and bazaars. Women adorn themselves with mehndi (henna) and dress in vibrant Shalwar Kameez. Sheer Khurma (sweet vermicelli) is the iconic Eid dish. Families visit graveyards to pray for the departed.

🌿 Mehndi🍮 Sheer Khurma🛍️ Bazaars
🇮🇩

Indonesia

The world's largest Muslim nation celebrates Lebaran (Eid) with Mudik — millions travel home for family reunions. Ketupat (rice cakes) and Rendang are shared. Mosques host massive open-air prayers, and communities exchange forgiveness with heartfelt "Maaf Lahir Batin".

🚂 Mudik🍛 Rendang🤲 Forgiveness
🇹🇷

Turkey

Turkey's Şeker Bayramı (Sugar Festival) sees children receive sweets and Baklava from every home they visit. Families kiss elders' hands in deep respect. Streets are illuminated, mosques broadcast beautiful Minarets' calls, and Turkish coffee flows freely.

🍬 Sweets🧁 Baklava☕ Coffee
🇲🇾

Malaysia

Hari Raya in Malaysia transforms cities with dazzling "open house" traditions where all races are welcomed. Satay, Lemang (bamboo rice), and Kuih pastries fill tables. Traditional Baju Melayu outfits shine brilliantly as families visit neighbors.

🏡 Open House🥩 Satay👘 Baju Melayu
🇬🇧

United Kingdom

British Muslims celebrate Eid in a beautifully multicultural setting. East London's Whitechapel and Birmingham's Sparkhill come alive with street bazaars, food stalls, and fairground rides. The Regent's Park Mosque hosts thousands for Eid prayers amid a diverse community.

🎪 Street Fairs🕌 Regent's Park🌍 Multicultural
🇫🇷

France

France's 5+ million Muslims celebrate Eid Al Ftr with great community spirit. The Grande Mosquée de Paris overflows with worshippers. North African communities bring vibrant Algerian, Moroccan, and Tunisian traditions, filling streets with incredible aromas of couscous and pastries.

🕌 Grande Mosquée🥘 Couscous🤲 Community
🇩🇪

Germany

Germany's Turkish-origin Muslim community hosts spectacular Eid gatherings. The DITIB mosque in Cologne draws thousands. Baklava, Börek, and Turkish sweets are shared with neighbors. Muslim children dress in beautiful new clothes and visit relatives across the city.

🕌 DITIB Mosque🍰 Börek👨‍👩‍👧 Family
🇦🇺

Australia

Australia's diverse Muslim community celebrates Eid with multicultural vibrancy. Lakemba in Sydney transforms into a festival city with food stalls from Lebanon, Pakistan, Egypt, and Turkey. The Lakemba Mosque hosts one of Australia's largest Eid prayers. Families of all backgrounds unite.

🏙️ Lakemba🌏 Multicultural🕌 Sydney Mosque
🦘

Melbourne

Melbourne's Islamic community hosts grand Eid prayer events in Flemington Racecourse. The city's multicultural spirit shines as Arabs, Somalis, Afghans, and Southeast Asians celebrate together. Community BBQs under the open skies make Australian Eid uniquely warm and welcoming.

🎊 Flemington🍖 Community BBQ⭐ Unity
❄️

Antarctica — Eid At The Edge Of The World

Even in the most remote and frozen corners of Earth, the spirit of Eid lives on. Muslim researchers and scientists stationed at polar research bases observe Eid with quiet spirituality and deep reflection. When the moon is sighted thousands of miles away, they pause their work, roll out their prayer rugs on frozen ground, and face the Qibla together. Perhaps only 3 or 4 souls in a room — yet the connection to over 1.8 billion Muslims worldwide makes the ice feel warm. They share whatever halal food they've packed, recite Takbeer into the Antarctic winds, and remind themselves that Islam unites every corner of this Earth — even the coldest, remotest, most beautiful one. Subhanallah.

🧊 Polar Prayer 🌙 Moon Sighting ❤️ Spiritual Unity 🤲 Eid Under Aurora
🍽️ Eid Cuisine

Eid Food Showcase

Hover over each dish to discover its story and cultural heritage. Food is love made edible!

🍚
Jollof Rice
West Africa
The crown jewel of West African cuisine! Slow-cooked tomato-based rice with fragrant spices. Nigerian Eid feasts are incomplete without this beloved dish. The smoky "party jollof" is legendary!
🥩
Suya
Nigeria
Smoky, spiced skewered meat grilled over open flame. Suya is marinated in a blend of groundnut, ginger, paprika, and spices — a sizzling Eid street favourite across Northern Nigeria.
🍛
Biryani
South Asia
The king of rice dishes! Aromatic basmati layered with spiced meat, saffron, and caramelized onions. Pakistan & India's Eid tables are never complete without this fragrant masterpiece.
🍲
Kabsa
Saudi Arabia
Saudi Arabia's national dish and Eid centrepiece. Long-grain rice cooked in a rich meat broth with cloves, cardamom, rose water, and saffron. Served on a massive communal platter with dates on the side.
🌴
Dates
Arabian Peninsula
The Sunnah food of the Prophet ﷺ. Every Muslim home breaks fast and opens Eid celebrations with dates. Sweet, chewy, nourishing — dates symbolize the sweetness of faith and blessings of Allah.
🥮
Baklava
Turkey / Middle East
Layers of crispy filo pastry filled with pistachios or walnuts, drenched in golden honey syrup. A Turkish Eid classic that's gifted in beautiful ornate boxes between families and neighbors.
🍮
Kunafa
Levant
A heavenly shredded pastry dessert soaked in orange blossom syrup, filled with stretchy cheese or cream. Palestinian and Lebanese families serve this warm, drizzled with pistachios on Eid mornings.
🥟
Samosa
South Asia / East Africa
Golden crispy triangles filled with spiced potato, lentils, or minced meat. From Pakistan to Kenya, samosas are the ultimate Eid snack — impossible to eat just one! Served with tangy chutney.
